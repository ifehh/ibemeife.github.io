FAQ'S

What curriculum do you provide?

We run a dual curriculum; Nigerian and British curriculums. The Nigerian curriculum prepares learners to understand, resolve problems, and make decisions. It is based on the approved National Education Policy. The British curriculum, on the other hand, with its international perspective, and outcome-based education approach, assists learners to function on the higher cognitive domains of learning, expecting learners to: Identify and solve problems, using and applying critical, creative and innovative thinking skills.

            How does the school handle discipline of...

            *At Beautiful Beginnings Schools, we use reward system to encourage positive behaviour which will discourage negative behaviour. We do not believe in punishment, our focus is on discipline and prompting positive attitude.

            How much homework do you have...?

            There is a functional homework schedule which is well supervised. It enables the system to operate with high standards.

            What library resources are...

            Beautiful Beginnings Schools has a physical library with over 200 books.

            How is technology used to support teaching and learning...

            Every classroom has a functional interactive board and air-conditioner. Learning is fun at Beautiful Beginnings Schools.

            What professional development opportunities do teachers have?

            Teachers are exposed to professional development programmes within and outside the school. There are regular in-service trainings and external programmes depending on the general or individual needs of the staff members

            What are the school hours?

            A typical school day starts at 7.50 a.m and ends at 2:00pm

            Do you offer school bus services in your day schools?

            We provide optional bus services for our students, with costs separate from the school fees. The bus service fees vary depending on the covered routes, offering convenience and flexibility for our students and parents.
